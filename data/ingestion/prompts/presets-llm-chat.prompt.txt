## About The Strength Period
The Strength Period is a client-side strength training application that generates and manages
periodized training plans. All data runs locally in the user's browser (no backend).
Presets are reusable training plan templates that the app uses to scaffold sessions for a user
over a defined number of weeks, combining exercises into a coherent, progressive program.

## Dual Engine Mode
The app has two planning modes, determined by preset shape:

### Generator mode (muscleDistribution only)
- Preset defines `muscleDistribution` percentages — the engine picks exercises from the user's
  filtered pool automatically.
- Volume is calculated from the user's time budget and distribution weights.
- Deload weeks are generated automatically at every 4th week at 60%.
- Use for general-purpose or adaptive presets where the specific exercises don't matter.

### Faithful mode (sessions[] present)
- Preset defines `sessions[]` with explicit exercises, sets, reps, rest, tempo, RPE.
- The engine follows the preset faithfully — it does NOT substitute exercises or invent volume.
- Deload sessions are defined by the preset (`isDeload: true`) — the engine does not auto-generate them.
- The engine only applies `weeklyProgression` scaling on top of the preset base.
- Use for opinionated, program-specific presets where exercise selection and order matter.

**Decision logic:** If `sessions` is present and non-empty → faithful mode. Otherwise → generator mode.
A preset should use ONE mode. Do not include both `sessions` and `muscleDistribution` in the same preset
(muscleDistribution is ignored when sessions are present).

## weeklyProgression
Scale from 0 to 10 that controls how aggressively the engine increases volume week over week:
- 0 = maintenance (multiplier stays 1.0, no progression)
- 5 = moderate progression (default)
- 10 = full progression rate
The engine scales sets/reps on top of the preset's base values using this factor.
Set this based on the training goal: rehab/maintenance presets → low (1–3), standard → 5, aggressive → 7–10.

## sessions[]
An array of session templates (A, B, C, etc.) that define the training structure:
- Each session has a `label` (e.g. "Session A", "Lower Body", "Full Body + Deload")
- Each session contains `exercises[]` with specific programming
- Number of sessions = implicit training frequency for this preset
- Optionally include deload sessions with `isDeload: true`
- The LLM has freedom to include deload sessions or not; if training rationale warrants them,
  add one or more sessions with `isDeload: true` and reduced volume/intensity

## tempo
Format: "eccentric-pause_bottom-concentric-pause_top" in seconds.
Example: "3-1-0-1" means 3s eccentric, 1s pause at bottom, explosive concentric, 1s pause at top.
Optional field. Include when the tempo is meaningful for the training goal (e.g., eccentric work,
time under tension). Omit when standard tempo is fine.

## rpe (Rate of Perceived Exertion)
Scale from 1 to 10. Optional. Include when the preset targets specific intensity zones.

## Target user profile
- Recreational and trail runners (tags: corredor, pujada, baixada are highly relevant)
- Athletes complementing running with gym-free strength work
- Users with common overuse injuries: knee tendinopathies, ankle issues, low back
- Beginner to intermediate training age

## Scientific rigor & scope
- This tool provides general strength training programming for runners. It is NOT a medical device, therapeutic tool, or substitute for professional medical advice.
- All training rationale must be grounded in established exercise science (progressive overload, specificity, recovery, periodization). No bro-science or unsubstantiated claims.
- Preset descriptions and notes MUST use conditional/conservative language: "may help improve", "evidence suggests", "can contribute to", "is associated with".
- NEVER use absolute terms: "cures", "fixes", "eliminates pain", "guarantees". NEVER promise therapeutic outcomes.
- Rehab-tagged presets must be especially conservative — recommend consulting a healthcare professional (physiotherapist, sports medicine doctor) when restrictions exceed the scope of general strength training.
