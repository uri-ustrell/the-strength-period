## About The Strength Period
The Strength Period is a client-side strength training application that generates and manages
periodized training plans. All data runs locally in the user's browser (no backend).
The app focuses on runners and endurance athletes who want to complement their sport with
structured strength work, including gamification and progress tracking.

## How exercises are used
- Exercises are assigned to training presets and plans. Presets can reference exercises directly
  (faithful mode with sessions[]) or let the engine pick from a filtered pool (generator mode).
- `progressionMetric` determines how the engine progresses the exercise over weeks:
  weight (add load), reps (add reps), seconds (hold longer).
- `equipment` determines which users can do the exercise (filtered by user's available equipment).
- `tags` help filter exercises for specific training goals (e.g., corredor, pujada, rehab_genoll).
- `restrictions` prevent exercises from being assigned to users with certain conditions
  (e.g., rehab_genoll → avoid/modify exercises that stress the knee).

## Target user profile
- Recreational runners and trail runners (corredor, pujada, baixada tags are highly relevant)
- Athletes with common overuse injuries (tendinitis rotuliana, anserina, rehab_genoll)
- Users training without a gym: bodyweight and minimal equipment preferred
  (pes_corporal, manueles, banda_elastica prioritized over machines)

## Scientific rigor & scope
- This tool provides general strength training programming for runners. It is NOT a medical device, therapeutic tool, or substitute for professional medical advice.
- Exercise descriptions, instructions, and notes must use evidence-based language grounded in biomechanical and exercise science principles. No bro-science or unsubstantiated claims.
- NEVER use absolute terms: "cures", "fixes", "eliminates pain", "guarantees". Use conditional language: "may help improve", "evidence suggests", "can contribute to".
- NEVER promise therapeutic outcomes. Exercises adapt training based on user-declared restrictions, not diagnose conditions.
- Exercise categorization (muscles, level, restrictions) must be based on established biomechanical principles.
- When there is doubt about whether an exercise should have a restriction, err on the side of caution — add the restriction with action "avoid" or "modify".
